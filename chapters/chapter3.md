With no answer at the door, the boys circle the side of the house. The garden is long unkempt - choked with dead plants and overgrown weeds. Not that it matters in a world like this.

A rusty hinge squeaks as Hayato enters through the opening, pushing the window upward to make room for his back.

'Ouch.'

The older boy winces as a sharp, digging sensation stabs his shoulder.

"Hayato, can you hurry up? Your feet are really hurting my shoulders."

The younger brother stops, turning back to face him.

"Yeah, I can!"

'This idiot.'

A grunt escapes Ko’s mouth as he clenches his teeth.

"HEY! That doesn't mean you have to answer!"

Hayato giggles as he slides into the quiet house, reaching an arm back out the window to help his brother inside.

As Ko is pulled through the frame, the raw scrape on his leg rubs against the wall, leaving him in an even more sour mood.

'I really have to put a bandage on that or something... yeouch.'

Now inside but not yet on his feet, he notices his brother's hand slipping.

"ARE YOU GIVING - "

He falls flat on his face, the carpet welcoming him with a soft embrace. He stands up, wondering what he could have possibly done to make whatever god is out there so angry today.

" - Up."

Feeling guilty, Hayato tucks his hands behind his back as a bead of sweat rolls down his forehead. He shuffles into a fake whistling posture, pointedly ignoring his aching brother.

"Nice one, Hayato."

'Time to go check on Mom and Dad...'

A look of disdain pulls at the boy’s mouth as he imagines what he’ll discover in their filthy house.

Opening the bedroom door pushes a mountain of trash across the hallway floor. A foul, squelching noise follows, accompanied by a strange, watery substance.

'Ew... nasty...'

"Hayato, just stay here, okay? I’ll go deal with this. Get some sleep."

Relieved, the younger brother lets out a long breath - thankful that Ko isn't too angry, and even more grateful he’s willing to handle the mess alone.

'Man, this is some shitty luck. I thought adoptive parents were supposed to provide decent homes... whatever.'

Walking into the living room, he can just make out his mother sprawled on the couch. Her matted brown hair looks greasy - clearly, she hasn’t taken care of herself in a long while.

Approaching the couch, he notices how shallow her breathing has become. Her skin feels cold to the touch, and the room smells faintly of neglect and stale air. He carefully moves her into a more comfortable position, his face hardening as he looks around the disheveled room.

'It's a mess. How did things get this bad? Do people really lose themselves this much?'

A sudden, loud crash from upstairs makes him flinch. He suspects it is his father, likely stumbling over the clutter and empty bottles that litter the floor above.

Heavy, uneven footsteps begin to descend the stairs, accompanied by a low, rhythmic grunting.

"W-W-Who's the-re...?"

The man's voice is thick and slurred, the words barely intelligible. He seems to be struggling just to stay upright as he reaches the bottom of the stairs.

The boy remains perfectly still and silent, hoping to avoid drawing the attention of his drunk father.

"Damned brats... Is that y-ou?"

'People do anything to distract themselves from life now. It’s sickening.'

The boy’s presence has fully captured his father’s attention. The man’s face is a mottled, venomous red.

"Come here, boy."

He slams the bottle he was carrying onto the side table, the glass ringing with the force.

"I said _come here_."

The terrified boy inches forward, his gaze glued to the filthy floor.

"What? Can’t even look me in the eye?"

Stopping just inches from the drunk, Ko shakes his head. His unkempt hair falls forward like a curtain, shielding his eyes as he continues to stare at the ground.

"Do you think you're better than me?"

A heavy blow strikes Ko, sending him sprawling. His cheek stings sharply and his vision blurs as he hits the floor. Above him, his father stands huffing - the air thick with the smell of liquor.

The man looms over the boy, his shadow stretching across the room. Ko remains on the ground, trembling, as the weight of his father's presence fills the space with dread. The silence is broken only by the man's heavy, uneven breathing and the rattle of the bottle on the side table.

'Dickhead.'

"Still nothing?"

A wide, sharp grin spreads across the man's face.

"Hayato - come here!"

The younger boy slowly pushes the door open, the creak echoing through the house. Each step he takes adds to the heavy dread in Ko's heart.

"Yes, Dad?"

The father’s grin grows wider, almost inhuman.

Ko gathers his strength, desperate to keep his brother away.

"Just go back to the room, Hayato. Everything's okay."

The man mockingly laughs.

"So now you want to talk? Hayato, I said come here - _now_."

Ko latches onto his fathers leg, his teeth digging into his ankle, biting through the stained jeans. His face stomped on in the process, then kicked to the side.
